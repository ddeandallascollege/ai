Key Concepts to Explain

The Attention Economy: Tech apps treat human attention as a product. The longer your eyes stay on a screen, the more money those companies generate through advertising space.
The Hook Model (Nir Eyal): Trigger → Action → Variable Reward (mimicking a slot machine) → Investment. Social feeds scrolling is designed exactly like casino mechanics to spark dopamine loops.
Cognitive Costs: A University of Chicago study showed that simply having a smartphone on your desk—even turned off—drains valuable working memory and problem-solving power.

Guided Exercises for Each Section:

Tab 1 (Explore): Guide students through the concept cards. Click through each key pillar of wellness together.

Tab 2 (Focus): Ask students to honestly write down their expected screen hours, then pull up their settings to find actual screen times. The results will shock them.

Tab 3 (Breathe): Run a 2-minute class Box Breathing exercise. Dim your projector screen if possible, turn on sound, and count together.

Tab 4 (Quiz): Have students privately complete the screener based on real clinical metrics. It removes shame by showing them they aren't alone.

Tab 5 (Action Plan): End with a written promise. Ask 2-3 volunteers to share their customized action plan commitments.

Interactive Seminar Outline

Can we control our screens before they control us?

In today's lecture, we're uncovering the mechanics of digital attention. This active platform guides us through reflecting on our psychological habits, evaluating cognitive costs, and practicing concrete physical tools to reclaim your attention and time.

01

Reclaiming Real Sleep

The blue light from phones mimics daytime. Even worse, notifications stress our brains right before bedtime. Designating a tech-free 40 minutes before sleep helps naturally normalize our circadian sleep patterns.

02

The Power of Presence

Studies prove that simply removing a phone from the physical table when having lunch with a friend increases memory retention, feelings of active listening, and relational trust.

03

Rewiring Dopamine

Social feeds are built like digital slot machines. Taking deliberate, regular spaces for mental silence restores natural dopamine baselines, increasing focus and real-world interest.
